**Prompt**: What are the products of System 2 thinking in AGI, and how can Prolog, knowledge graphs, and LLMs be combined for advanced reasoning in the post-LLM era?

**Abstract**: The blog post examines the outputs of System 2—deliberate, analytical reasoning—in an AGI "Assemblage of AI" architecture. Rather than focusing on cognitive processes, it highlights the structured artifacts System 2 produces: reusable plans, procedures, models, stories, playbooks, and strategies that encode knowledge for the Enterprise Knowledge Graph. Intelligence is framed as pattern recognition (System 1/LLMs) combined with analogical remixing of existing solutions from evolution, mimicry, and culture, rather than invention from nothing. In the LLM era, LLMs excel at detecting relational patterns and generating analogies, while Prolog provides powerful logical reasoning capabilities—including recursion, backtracking, and narrative simulation—over RDF/OWL knowledge graphs. This hybrid system uses LLMs to produce RDF triples or Prolog clauses, SPARQL for graph queries, and Prolog for executable logic superior to SWRL. Enterprise vector memory aids fuzzy retrieval of these artifacts. The author advocates cautious integration to augment human System 2 thinking, preserving agency amid AI adoption.

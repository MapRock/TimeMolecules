Prompt: How can I use intersegment event analysis to find external signals, marker events, spikes, or unrelated activity that may explain why a process segment became slow or anomalous?

Abstract: Intersegment event analysis is used when a process segment looks unusual and the likely explanation may lie outside the modeled workflow itself. Instead of looking only at the cases that make up the slow transition, this pattern widens the analysis to all events that occurred during the same time window. That broader view can surface external influences such as traffic, outages, maintenance, workload surges, environmental disruptions, or activity from other domains. It can also reveal computed marker events such as spikes, inflection points, threshold breaches, and anomaly flags generated by CEP or related logic. These events matter because they may indicate that the surrounding system changed state, crossed a threshold, or came under pressure at the same moment the segment became abnormal. This helps distinguish internal process failure from outside influence and gives a fuller basis for diagnosing delays and interpreting anomalous behavior. ([GitHub][1])

Prompt: Why are Markov models fundamentally important for understanding stories, sequences, and process-aware intelligence in Time Molecules? 

Abstract: Stories are meaningful sequences of events, and they are the transactional unit of human-level intelligence because experience, explanation, planning, and memory are all organized through ordered flows rather than isolated facts. In enterprise data, those stories appear as cases such as customer journeys, patient visits, service incidents, and AI agent runs. Markov models are fundamentally important because they abstract many similar stories into a compact process memory that preserves what matters most: which events tend to follow others, how likely those transitions are, where branches and loops occur, and how long transitions tend to take. This makes Markov models more than prediction tools. They are abstractions of process experience that let Time Molecules aggregate stories about processes unfolding through time, complementing OLAP’s aggregation of facts about things.

* **Prompt**: What are Time Molecules and how can they transform Business Intelligence through process mining and systems thinking?
* **Abstract**: The post previews the book *Time Molecules: The BI Side of Process Mining and Systems Thinking*. It presents Time Molecules as a new analytical construct designed as the temporal counterpart to traditional OLAP cubes. Defined as vast collections of probabilistic Hidden Markov Models (HMMs) across multi-dimensional spaces, Time Molecules efficiently compress millions to trillions of event sequences into compact probabilistic models of process flows and transition patterns. This enables BI systems to move beyond static metric aggregation ("what happened") toward dynamic process understanding ("how and why did this sequence occur" and "what is likely to happen next"). Built on principles from process mining and systems thinking, Time Molecules offer scalable, interpretable, and cacheable representations of real-world processes. The framework integrates with existing BI infrastructure and the author's broader Enterprise Intelligence architecture, providing both conceptual foundations and a practical SQL Server implementation for turning raw event data into actionable process intelligence.

Prompt: How do the books Enterprise Intelligence and Time Molecules relate to each other?

Abstract: Enterprise Intelligence (2024) introduces the Enterprise Knowledge Graph built around the Insight Space Graph and the Tuple Correlation Web (TCW) to extend traditional BI with tuple-based correlation analysis across time. Time Molecules (2025) builds process-aware intelligence through case-based event data, Markov models, and probabilistic transition graphs. The two books connect powerfully through the Tuple Correlation Web: when the TCW from Enterprise Intelligence is combined with the Markov models of Time Molecules, it forms a web of Hidden Markov Models (HMMs). This integration creates a unified Enterprise Knowledge Graph (EKG) that captures both tuple correlations (co-movements of metrics/events) and process transitions, giving enterprises a practical “BI version of space-time” for analyzing both things/concepts and dynamic processes/systems. Time Molecules is designed to be read independently but is conceptually and technically the direct successor that extends the foundational TCW work.

Prompt: What are the key differences between public (external API) LLMs and private LLMs in enterprise settings, and when should an organization choose a hybrid, public, or private approach?

Abstract: For enterprise intelligence, public (external) LLMs accessed via secured APIs are the default choice for general reasoning, creative synthesis, public knowledge, drafting, and non-sensitive tasks because they deliver frontier-level performance at low cost with minimal operational burden and strong default privacy guarantees (no training on your data). Private LLMs (self-hosted open-weights models or managed private deployments) are reserved for ultra-sensitive domains such as competitive strategies, M&A plans, classified data, regulatory/healthcare information, or proprietary manufacturing logic where maximum data sovereignty and isolation are required. Eugene strongly recommends a hybrid model: own the sovereign RAG layer (Enterprise Knowledge Graph, documents, Prolog rules, Time Molecules, etc.) in all cases so proprietary knowledge never leaves your governed boundary, while using external LLMs for “public cognition” and private LLMs only for “strategic cognition.”

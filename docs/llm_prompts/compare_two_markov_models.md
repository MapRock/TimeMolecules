Prompt: How does the `dbo.InsertModelSimilarities` stored procedure compare two Markov models in TimeSolution using `dbo.ModelEvents` and store the results in `dbo.ModelSimilarity`, including its parameters, overlap metrics, cosine similarity, and optional segment display?

Abstract: `dbo.InsertModelSimilarities` compares two existing stored Markov models by reading their transition segments from `dbo.ModelEvents`, normalizing the model ID order, and calculating multiple similarity measures before updating or inserting a row in `dbo.ModelSimilarity`. Its inputs are `@ModelID1`, `@ModelID2`, and `@DisplaySegments`. The procedure counts each model’s segments, computes the combined unique segment count, derives a Jaccard-like `PercentSameSegments` based on shared `EventA -> EventB` pairs, calculates `CosineSimilarity` from matching transition probabilities, and computes `SameSegments_ttest` from differences in average transition times using stored variance and row counts. When `@DisplaySegments = 1`, it also returns a segment-level comparison of shared transitions, including probabilities, averages, and row counts for both models. This enables persistent, order-insensitive comparison of two stored models for later retrieval and ranking.

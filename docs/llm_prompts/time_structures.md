Prompt: What are the three main time-based relationships in Time Molecules, and how do they relate to understanding processes over time?

Abstract: Time Molecules centers on three complementary ways of observing change through time. Markov models answer “what happens next?” by measuring transition probabilities between events across many cases of a process. Conditional probabilities answer “under these conditions, how likely is it?” by estimating the likelihood of an event given a surrounding context or set of accompanying factors. Correlations answer “do these things move together over time?” by measuring how variables or event-related metrics rise and fall together across time intervals. Markov models and conditional probabilities together form the two main parts of a Hidden Markov Model: one captures transitions between states, and the other captures the likelihood of observed events under those states. Correlation operates alongside them as a discovery signal, pointing toward relationships worth testing or modeling further. ([Soft Coded Logic][1])
